Clustering Methods Suite

Comprehensive collection of clustering algorithms organized by approach

Clustering is an unsupervised machine learning technique that groups similar data points together based on their characteristics, without requiring prior knowledge of the groups. It helps identify patterns, structures, and relationships in data by organizing it into meaningful clusters where points within the same cluster are more similar to each other than to points in other clusters.

Clustering algorithms are broadly categorized into three main approaches, each with its own strengths and use cases:

Clustering
Partitioning
Density-Based
Hierarchical

Partitioning methods divide data into k clusters by optimizing a criterion function. They include centroid-based approaches (K-means, K-Medoids), model-based methods (GMM, EM), and graph-based techniques (Spectral, Louvain).

Density-based methods identify clusters as dense regions of data points separated by sparse regions. They excel at finding clusters of arbitrary shapes and handling noise, including DBSCAN, HDBSCAN, OPTICS, and DENCLUE.

Hierarchical methods build a tree-like structure (dendrogram) representing cluster relationships. They can be agglomerative (bottom-up) or divisive (top-down), with different linkage criteria like Single, Complete, Average, and Ward's Method.
