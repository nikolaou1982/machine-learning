Anomaly Detection Methods Suite

Comprehensive collection of anomaly detection algorithms organized by approach

Anomaly Detection is an unsupervised machine learning technique that identifies unusual patterns, outliers, or data points that deviate significantly from expected behavior. It helps detect fraud, system intrusions, manufacturing defects, and other rare events that don't conform to normal patterns in the data.

Anomaly detection algorithms are broadly categorized into five main approaches, each with its own strengths and use cases:

Distance-based methods identify anomalies as data points that are far from their nearest neighbors or cluster centers. They use distance metrics like Euclidean, Manhattan, or Mahalanobis distance to measure how unusual a point is.

Density-based methods detect anomalies in regions of low density. Points that lie in sparse areas compared to their neighbors are flagged as anomalies. These methods are effective for non-uniform data distributions.

Ensemble methods combine multiple anomaly detection algorithms to improve detection accuracy and robustness. They aggregate results from different models to make more reliable anomaly predictions.

Neural Networks use deep learning architectures like autoencoders, variational autoencoders, and GANs to learn normal patterns and identify deviations. They excel at detecting complex, high-dimensional anomalies.

Probabilistic methods model the probability distribution of normal data and identify points with low probability as anomalies. They include statistical methods, mixture models, and Bayesian approaches.
